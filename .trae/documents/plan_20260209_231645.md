I have identified the cause of the issue. The navigation buttons (arrows) in the "Original Preview" modal move with the page because of a CSS interaction between `fixed` positioning and `backdrop-filter`.

The modal container uses `backdrop-blur-md` (which applies `backdrop-filter`). In modern browsers, applying a filter to an element makes it the "containing block" for all its fixed-positioned descendants. This means the buttons, which are set to `fixed`, become positioned relative to the scrolling container rather than the viewport. When you zoom in and scroll/pan the page, the buttons scroll along with it.

To fix this, I will refactor the `OriginalPreviewModal` component to separate the background/filter effect from the scroll container.

### Plan:
1.  **Refactor `OriginalPreviewModal.tsx`**:
    *   Change the root structure to a wrapper `div` that acts as the positioning context but does not have the `backdrop-filter` or scroll properties.
    *   Move the background and backdrop blur to a separate `div` that is absolutely positioned.
    *   Move the navigation buttons (Left/Right arrows) to be direct children of the root wrapper. This ensures they remain fixed relative to the viewport and are unaffected by the scrolling of the content.
    *   Keep the existing logic (zoom, pan, interactions) on the inner container `div`, which will remain the scroll target.

This change will ensure the buttons stay anchored to the screen edges regardless of zoom level or panning, while preserving all existing functionality.
